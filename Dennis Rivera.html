About Dennis Rivera

Dennis Rivera is a legendary figure in American labor history, known for his charismatic leadership and strategic brilliance in building one of the most powerful healthcare unions in the United States. His influence extends far beyond the labor movement into national politics and healthcare policy.

Core Identity

A Puerto Rican-born labor union leader who revolutionized healthcare unionism and became a key political ally for the Democratic Party, leveraging worker power to advance progressive causes.

Throughout his career, Rivera has demonstrated an exceptional ability to mobilize workers, influence policy, and build coalitions that have shaped American healthcare and labor rights.

Leadership at 1199SEIU United Healthcare Workers East

Rivera's most significant impact came during his tenure as president of 1199SEIU, the largest healthcare union in the United States.

Presidency: 1989-2007

During his 18-year leadership, Rivera transformed 1199SEIU into a political and organizing powerhouse:

  • Dramatic Growth: Expanded the union's membership and influence significantly
  • Strategic Organizing: Mastered hospital and nursing home worker organizing
  • Political Mobilization: Developed the ability to mobilize tens of thousands of members for political actions
  • The "1199 Magic": His charismatic leadership made the union's endorsement highly sought after by politicians at all levels

Political Influence and Key Issues

Rivera made healthcare workers' union a dominant force in progressive politics, focusing on several key issues:

Healthcare Reform

He was a pivotal early and forceful supporter of the Affordable Care Act (Obamacare), using his union's resources and grassroots network to help pass the landmark legislation.

Medicaid & Medicare

Consistently fought to protect and expand these crucial programs, which were vital for both his members' patients and their job security in the healthcare sector.

State and Local Politics

In New York, Rivera was instrumental in advocating for progressive legislation including:

  • Healthcare staffing ratios to ensure quality patient care
  • Workers' rights and fair labor practices
  • Increased funding for healthcare services
  • His endorsement was often seen as essential for Democrats running for statewide office

Later Roles & Continued Influence

After stepping down from the 1199SEIU presidency in 2007, Rivera remained a major player in national politics and the labor movement.

2007-

Chair of SEIU Healthcare

Served as the first chair of SEIU Healthcare, coordinating healthcare initiatives for the entire SEIU international union.

2007-

Senior Advisor to SEIU President

Acted as a key strategic advisor to SEIU's international president, Andy Stern.

2007

Coalition Building

Founded the Partnership for Quality Care (PQC), a coalition of healthcare providers and unions working to improve the U.S. healthcare system.

2008-2020

National Politics

Prominent surrogate and advisor for Democratic presidential campaigns, including Hillary Clinton and Joe Biden, focusing on Latino outreach and healthcare policy.
