Roger Ward - Australian Actor

Roger Ward is a quintessential figure in the Australian New Wave cinema of the 1970s and 80s, internationally recognized for his role in Mad Max.

Vital Information

Born: 1938 in Adelaide, South Australia

Profession: Actor, Stuntman, Author

Known For: Tough, rugged, or villainous characters in Australian cult cinema

Notable Roles & Career

Mad Max (1979)

Best known for his role as "Fifi" Macaffee, the cynical and bureaucratic Captain of the Main Force Patrol. This role made him a recognizable face in cult cinema worldwide.

The Man from Hong Kong (1975)

Played a key role in this early Australia-Hong Kong co-production, helping to establish his reputation in action cinema.

Turkey Shoot (1982)

Starred in this notorious and violent exploitation film, which has developed a strong cult following over the years.

Television Career

Long career on Australian TV with appearances in classic series like Prisoner (as Officer Spence), The Sullivans, Homicide, and Bluey.

Beyond acting, Ward has also worked as a stuntman and has written several books about his life and career in the film industry.

Roger Ward - American Racing Driver

Roger Ward was a legend in American open-wheel racing, particularly during the 1950s and 1960s, with two Indianapolis 500 victories to his name.

Profession: Auto Racing Driver

Notable Achievements & Career

Indianapolis 500 Champion

Won the prestigious Indianapolis 500 twice (1959 and 1962). Winning this race once makes a driver famous; winning it twice cements their legacy in motorsports history.

National Champion

Was the USAC National Champion in 1959, the same year as his first Indianapolis 500 victory.

Competitive Rivalry

Was a fierce competitor and rival to other legends of the era, most notably A.J. Foyt, creating one of the great rivalries in American motorsports.

Versatility

While his greatest success came on paved ovals, he was also a skilled road racer and competed in the NASCAR Grand National Series (now Cup Series).

He is a member of the Motorsports Hall of Fame of America and the National Sprint Car Hall of Fame, cementing his legacy as one of America's racing greats.
