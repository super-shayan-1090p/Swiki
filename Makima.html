Character Overview

Makima is the primary antagonist of Tatsuki Fujimoto's dark fantasy manga "Chainsaw Man." She serves as the head of the Public Safety Devil Hunters and is later revealed to be the Control Devil, one of the Four Horsemen.

Her calm, collected demeanor masks a manipulative and terrifyingly powerful being who sees humans and devils as tools to be controlled.

The Art of Manipulation

Psychological Control

Makima excels at understanding and exploiting human psychology. She identifies people's deepest desires and fears, using them to establish complete control over her subordinates.

The Dog Metaphor

She views relationships in terms of dominance and submission, famously stating she wants to make "pets" of those she finds interesting, particularly Chainsaw Man.

Key Relationships

Denji

Her primary target of manipulation. She exploits his loneliness and desire for normalcy, becoming the object of his obsession while viewing him as a tool to control Chainsaw Man.

Aki Hayakawa

One of her most loyal subordinates whose tragic fate she orchestrates. She manipulates his desire for revenge and family, ultimately using him as a pawn in her plans.

Power

The Blood Devil who becomes Denji's friend. Makima eliminates her to emotionally break Denji and demonstrate her absolute control.

Kishibe

The most experienced devil hunter who sees through her manipulation but is powerless to stop her directly. He represents one of the few characters who understands her true nature.

"I want to make Chainsaw Man my dog. I want him to be mine and only mine."

Manipulation Methods

Love Bombing

She showers targets with affection and validation initially, creating dependency before gradually introducing control and manipulation.

Isolation

She systematically isolates her targets from their support systems, making them increasingly dependent on her for validation and companionship.

Future Faking

Makima promises a perfect future together while ensuring it remains just out of reach, keeping her targets striving for her approval.

Intermittent Reinforcement

She provides affection and rewards unpredictably, creating an addictive cycle where targets constantly seek to please her.

"If I told you to die, you would die. That's the kind of woman I am."

Psychological Profile

The Fear of Intimacy

Despite her desire for control over relationships, Makima fears genuine emotional connection, preferring the safety of complete dominance over vulnerability.

Messiah Complex

She genuinely believes her vision of a better world justifies any means necessary, seeing herself as the only one capable of creating true peace.

The Ultimate Narcissist

Makima sees others as extensions of herself rather than independent beings, unable to form genuine relationships based on mutual respect.

Fear of Being Equal

Her entire existence is built around maintaining superiority, as being seen as equal to others would destroy her self-concept.

"I've always been alone. I've never had anyone by my side. That's why I want to make Chainsaw Man mine."

Why Makima is Iconic

The Modern Antagonist

She represents a new type of villain - not motivated by traditional evil, but by a twisted desire for connection through control.

Psychological Horror

Her manipulation tactics feel terrifyingly real, representing real-world abusive relationship dynamics amplified through supernatural power.

Subversion of Expectations

She appears as a typical cool, competent female lead before revealing her true nature as one of manga's most terrifying antagonists.

Symbolic Depth

As the Control Devil, she represents humanity's fear of losing autonomy and being dominated by external forces.

"All devils fear Chainsaw Man. When he eats a devil, that devil ceases to exist in this world and in people's memories."

In short, Makima is the terrifyingly manipulative Control Devil of Chainsaw Man, whose calm exterior masks a being of absolute control and psychological manipulation, representing one of modern manga's most compelling antagonists.
