About Assemblymember Ann Gonzalez

Ann Gonzalez is a dedicated public servant and member of the Democratic Party who has represented California's 53rd Assembly District since her election in 2012. With multiple successful re-elections, she has established herself as a prominent figure in California politics.

Political Background

Before serving in the State Assembly, Ann Gonzalez was an elected member of the Glendale Community College Board of Trustees. She brings a background in both business and education to her legislative work.

Throughout her tenure, Assemblymember Gonzalez has focused on practical solutions to the challenges facing her constituents and all Californians. Her legislative record reflects her commitment to consumer rights, environmental sustainability, and educational opportunities.

Assembly District 53

Assemblymember Gonzalez represents the 53rd District of California, which covers a significant portion of Los Angeles County including diverse urban and suburban communities.

Policy Priorities & Legislative Work

Consumer Protection

As Chair of the Assembly Committee on Consumer Protection and Business, Assemblymember Gonzalez has been a leading voice for:

  • Data privacy and security legislation
  • Fair business practices and regulations
  • Product safety standards
  • Protection against fraudulent activities

Utilities & Energy

In her role as Chair of the Assembly Committee on Utilities and Energy, she oversees critical policy areas including:

  • California's transition to clean energy
  • Electricity, water, and gas infrastructure
  • Telecommunications policy
  • Climate change mitigation strategies

Additional Legislative Focus

Beyond her committee responsibilities, Assemblymember Gonzalez works on legislation related to:

  • Healthcare access and affordability
  • Education funding and reform
  • Support for working families
  • Economic development
